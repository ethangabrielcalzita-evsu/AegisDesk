Manager User Guide

Instructions for IT/SecOps managers to triage incidents, update NIST stages, and close tickets.

Your Role

As a manager, you have access to all incidents and can move tickets through the NIST incident response lifecycle. Your work supports secure, auditable incident handling.

1. Review the Incident Queue

Use the Incident Queue to see all open incidents that require triage or action.

  • Sort incidents by severity and submission time.
  • Identify critical or high-priority tickets first.
  • Check incident details to verify affected assets and reported symptoms.
2. Triage and Update Stages

Use the manager update form to move an incident through the NIST stages.

  • Preparation: Validate and classify the incident.
  • Detection & Analysis: Investigate and confirm scope.
  • Containment, Eradication & Recovery: Mitigate and remove threats.
  • Post-Incident Activity: Document lessons learned.
  • Closed: Mark resolved when the incident is complete.
3. Bulk Actions

When multiple incidents are resolved, use the bulk close form to update them all at once.

  • Select the tickets to close from the queue.
  • Confirm the stage and save the update.
  • All bulk changes are logged for audit purposes.
4. Use Manager Dashboard Metrics

The manager dashboard shows key operational data to help prioritize response activity.

  • Monitor open ticket totals and NIST stage distribution.
  • Watch critical/high severity counts and respond immediately.
  • Use the severity breakdown to balance workload and escalation.
5. Best Practices
  • Update stages promptly so employees can track progress.
  • Document actions clearly in ticket notes.
  • Close tickets only after all remediation steps are complete.
  • Use the audit log for accountability and compliance review.
