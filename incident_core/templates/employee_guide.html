Employee User Guide

Learn how to submit incidents, track ticket status, and manage your requests in AegisDesk.

Your Role

As an employee, you are responsible for reporting issues and monitoring the progress of your submitted incidents. You do not have access to the manager ticket queue or triage controls.

1. Submit a Ticket

Use the Submit Ticket page to report a new issue. Provide a clear title, detailed description, affected asset, category, and severity.

  • Choose the appropriate incident category.
  • Select the correct severity level.
  • Describe the impact and symptoms clearly.

Tip: Keep descriptions concise but include enough detail for the security team to triage the issue quickly.

2. Ticket Submission Rules

Incidents are rate limited to protect the system from abuse.

  • Maximum of 5 submissions per minute per IP address.
  • If you reach the limit, wait and submit again.
  • All tickets are logged for audit and review.

This ensures the service remains stable for all employees.

3. Track Your Tickets

Visit My Tickets to see all incidents you reported. Your ticket list shows current status, severity, and NIST response stage.

  • Open tickets are still being worked on.
  • Closed tickets have completed the incident response lifecycle.
  • Click a ticket ID to view detailed incident history, current stage, and related asset information.

If you need additional help, provide further updates through the ticket detail page or contact your IT/SecOps team directly.

4. Common Employee Workflows
  1. Submit a new incident when you detect a security event or IT issue.
  2. Check My Tickets for updates and current response stage.
  3. Review the ticket detail for notes and audit information.
  4. Wait for the security team to triage and respond.

Remember: Your submissions help the team respond faster and keep the environment secure.
